Sivroni S (2025). Temporal Dissociation Between Inter-Area and Local Neural Signals in Perceptual Choice. Zenodo. doi:10.5281/zenodo.17913638
Local population activity substantially outperforms inter-area correlations in predicting perceptual choice after stimulus onset (64.5% vs. 57.2%), while both contribute equally before stimulus. Analyzed simultaneous multi-region Neuropixels recordings, demonstrating a dual-mechanism model where pre-stimulus inter-area dynamics set the stage for post-stimulus local computation.
Published
Spivak L, Someck S, Levi A, Sivroni S, Stark E (2024). Wired together, change together: Spike timing modifies transmission in converging assemblies. Science Advances, 10:eadj4411
Spike timing of entire cell assemblies has a more substantial impact on effective connectivity than individual cell pairs. Using closed-loop optogenetic stimulation in CA1 of freely moving mice, unique spike patterns were generated between presynaptic pyramidal cells and postsynaptic parvalbumin cells, revealing an unexpected plasticity mechanism in converging assemblies.
Published
Sivroni S, Sloin HE, Stark E (2023). Short-term auditory priming in freely-moving mice. iScience, 26:107847
First behavioral model of short-term priming in lab animals. Establishes an auditory discrimination paradigm for studying response priming in freely moving mice, finding a priming effect in success rate driven by differential interference from incongruent stimuli. Opens the door to studying the cellular-network basis of priming.

Sloin HE, Bikovski L, Levi A, Amber-Vitos O, Katz T, Spivak L, Someck S, Gattegno R, Sivroni S, Sjulson L, Stark E (2022). Hybrid offspring of C57BL/6J mice exhibit improved properties for neurobehavioral research. eNeuro, 9:ENEURO.0221-22.2022
Hybrid FVB/NJ x C57BL/6J mice show reduced anxiety, improved learning, enhanced long-term spatial memory, and better sensorimotor performance with aging. Hybrids support optogenetic control and exhibit typical hippocampal rate and phase coding, providing an improved model for neurobehavioral studies.
